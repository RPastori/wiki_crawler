Heavy metal gallop

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search
For other uses, see Gallop.

A gallop is a beat or rhythm typically used in metal songs, eighth-sixteenth-sixteenth (), played on the rhythm guitar or drum kit (strum or drum pattern), mostly using a double kick pedal.

The gallop is usually formed around this skeleton:

H- x---x---x---x---|
S- ----o-------o---|
B- o-ooo-ooo-ooo-oo|

 Play (help·info)

This rhythmic figure may be used on palm muted power chords providing accompanimental rhythmic ostinati on the rhythm guitar ( Play example (help·info)[1]),[2][3] and may be heard in the introduction to Deep Purple's "Highway Star",[4] as well as their "Hard Lovin' Man" and Black Sabbath's "Children of the Grave" and "Air Dance".[5] Also Metallica's "Motorbreath" and, more famously, "The Four Horsemen",[6] as well as "Raining Blood" by Slayer[7] The pattern has also been used by groups such as Iron Maiden, Judas Priest, Black Sabbath and King Diamond.[1] Variations include triplet gallop rhythms ( Play (help·info)).[8]

This pattern has also been used in many non-metal songs. A classic example is the Scott Walker single "Jacky" released in 1967.[citation needed]
