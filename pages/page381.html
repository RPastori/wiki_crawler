Events[edit]

  • On April 28, 2015, Black Sabbath announced that they will embark on their final tour.[35]
  • On July 5, 2015, Blackmore's Night and former Deep Purple and Rainbow guitarist Ritchie Blackmore announced that he will briefly return to rock music by performing a few shows in June.[36]
  • On January 14, Lacuna Coil announced that guitarist Marco Biazzi has left the band.[37][38][39]
  • On February 10, Skeletonwitch announced Adam Clemans as their new vocalist.[40]
  • On February 20, Suicidal Tendencies announced Dave Lombardo (formerly of Slayer) as the drummer for their U.S. tour with Megadeth, which took place in February and March.[41] Lombardo will drum for Suicidal Tendencies again on their summer European tour.[42]
  • On March 19, Underoath played their first show since disbanding in 2013 at Self Help Festival in California.[43]
  • On March 31, Martin Henriksson announced that he has quit Dark Tranquillity due to loss of passion for playing music.[44]
  • On April 16 and April 23, Guns N' Roses reunited with their original guitarist Slash and bassist Duff McKagan for this year's Coachella.[45][46]
  • On April 16, Overkill played a special show in Oberhausen where they played the Feel the Fire and Horrorscope albums in their entirety. The show was professionally filmed and recorded for an upcoming DVD.[47]
  • On May 5, Eluveitie announced that they parted ways with drummer Merlin Sutter, vocalist Anna Murphy and guitarist Ivo Henzi.[48]
  • On August 6 and 7, Heavy Montreal took place presented by Evenko, and headliners were Nightwish, Disturbed, Five Finger Death Punch, Volbeat and Candlemass.[49]

Deaths[edit]

  • January 14 – Kevin Lawrence, guitarist of Rapidfire, died from multiple heart attacks at the age of 51.[50]
  • January 23 – Jimmy Bain, bassist of Rainbow and Dio, died from lung cancer at the age of 68.[51][52]
  • February 2 – Steve Childers, guitarist of Burning Inside, Black Witchery and Public Assassin, died in a car accident at the age of 49.[53]
  • February 21 – Piotr Grudziński, guitarist of Riverside, died from sudden cardiac arrest at the age of 40.[54][55][56]
  • March 3 – John Thomas, former guitarist of Budgie, died from undisclosed reasons at the age of 63.[57]
  • March 3 – Oskar Karlsson, drummer of Gates of Ishtar, died from undisclosed reasons at the age of 39.[58]
  • March 13 – Nik Green, keyboardist of Blue Murder, died from cancer.[59]
  • April 5 – John Byrd, former guitarist of King Conquer, died from substance abuse.[60]
  • April 18 – Aleah Liane Stanbridge, vocalist of Trees of Eternity, died from cancer.[61]
  • May 21 – Nick Menza, former drummer of Megadeth, died from heart failure at the age of 51.[62]
  • May 31 – Brandon Ferrell, former drummer of Municipal Waste, died from undisclosed reasons.[63]
  • June 9 – Adam Young, multi-instrumentalist of Sockweb, died after committing suicide by hanging himself at the age of 28.[14][64]
  • August 13 – Jerry Clyde Paradis, former drummer of The Gates of Slumber and Sourvein, died from undisclosed reasons.[65]
  • August 17 – James Woolley, former keyboardist of Nine Inch Nails, died from undisclosed reasons at the age of 49.[66]
  • August 21 – Tom Searle, guitarist of Architects, died from cancer at the age of 28.[67]
  • August 25 – Norman Killeen, former drummer of Threat Signal, died from undisclosed reasons at the age of 38.[68]
  • September 10 – Pecu Cinnari, drummer of Tarot, died from a long-term illness at the age of 50.[69]
  • September 11 – Leonard Haze, former drummer of Y&T, died from chronic obstructive pulmonary disease at the age of 61.[70]
  • September 27 – Mike Taylor, former vocalist of Quartz, died from undisclosed reasons.[71]
  • September 27 – Brodie Wheeler, former guitarist of King Conquer, died from substance abuse.[72]
  • December 5 – Adam Sagan, former drummer of Circle II Circle and Into Eternity, died from blood cancer at the age of 36.[73]
  • December 23 – Mick Zane, guitarist of Malice, died from brain cancer.[74]
